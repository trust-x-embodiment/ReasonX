Call for Contributions

We invite submissions to the CoRL 2025 Workshop on Reasoning as a Bridge: Generalizing Policies Across Robotic Embodiments. This workshop aims to bring together researchers interested in policy generalization, cross-embodiment reasoning, imitation learning, and scalable robot learning.

Topics of Interest

  • Reasoning and abstraction for cross-embodiment generalization
  • Imitation learning and policy transfer across different robot morphologies
  • Semantic, causal, or invariant representations for generalization
  • Language-based interfaces for policy reasoning
  • Foundation models for robotics (VLMs, LLMs, etc.)
  • Embodiment-aware perception, control, and policy learning
  • Benchmarks and evaluation methodologies for generalization

Submission Guidelines

We welcome short paper submissions (2–4 pages excluding references) in PDF format, formatted using the CoRL 2025 template. Submissions should not be anonymized.

Accepted contributions will be presented as spotlight talks or posters during the workshop. We encourage submission of works-in-progress, position papers, and novel ideas. Dual submission to CoRL 2025 or other conferences is allowed.
