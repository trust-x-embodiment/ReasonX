Overview

As robots are increasingly deployed in diverse and open-ended environments, building trustworthy policies is essential. In this workshop, we interpret trustworthiness as the integration of two core capabilities:

  • Safety: ensuring reliable and predictable behaviour under real-world conditions;
  • Generalization: enabling effective policy transfer across tasks, domains, and robotic embodiments.
These two pillars are foundational to achieving scalable, robust autonomy across a wide spectrum of platforms â€” including manipulators, quadrupeds, humanoids, aerial drones, mobile robots, and autonomous vehicles â€” each presenting distinct challenges in terms of dynamics, morphology, and sensory input. As the field progresses toward more generalist systems, ensuring that policies can adapt safely across such heterogeneous embodiments is crucial for real-world deployment at scale.

Recent frameworks for cross-embodiment policy frameworks (e.g., Ï€0, OpenVLA) demonstrate promising scalability. However, they often rely on domain-specific adaptation or data-intensive retraining, and seldom address the deeper question:

How do we build policies that generalize structurally and safely across diverse bodies?

We posit that reasoning is a key enabler of trustworthy cross-embodiment policy learning. Structured reasoningâ€”via causal world models, semantic abstractions, or intermediate representationsâ€”can help robots make decisions that are safe, interpretable, and transferrable across embodiments. Additionally, methods such as representation learning and morphology-aware policy design provide scalable tools for aligning behaviour across diverse embodiments.

Language, as a medium of abstraction and compositionality, further provides a natural interface for expressing transferable intent, constraints, and affordances. Vision-language and Vision-language-action models (e.g., VLMs, VLAs) hold great potential for bridging embodiment gaps through interpretable goal representation and instruction-following capabilities.

This workshop will investigate one central question: How can we build safe policies that generalize across robotic embodiments using reasoning and related scalable techniques as the interface?

Topics of interest include:
  • Structured Reasoning for Transfer: How can world models, causal reasoning, or chain-of-thought methods support cross-embodiment generalization?
  • Safety and Robustness: How do we ensure safe behavior when transferring policies across bodies with different control spaces or constraints?
  • Language and Foundation Models: How can language-based interfaces enhance interpretability and policy transfer?
  • Imitation Learning and Cross-Embodiment Skill Transfer: What scalable strategies enable transfer via demonstrations, distillation, or alignment?
  • Representation Learning: What embeddings or abstractions support morphology- and dynamics-invariant behaviors?
  • Evaluation and Benchmarking: How should we measure safety, trust, and generalization across embodiments, in simulation and the real world?

Keywords:
  • Cross-embodiment policy learning (imitation and skill transfer)
  • Reasoning in robot learning (semantic and causal abstraction)
  • Trustworthy autonomy (safety and generalization)
  • Foundation models for robotics (Vision-language(-action) models)
