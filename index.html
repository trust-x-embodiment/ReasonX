Overview

As robots are increasingly deployed in diverse and open-ended environments, building trustworthy policies is essential. In this workshop, we interpret trustworthiness as the integration of two core capabilities: safety — ensuring reliable and predictable behavior under real-world conditions — and generalization — enabling effective policy transfer across tasks, domains, and robotic embodiments. Together, these attributes are key to achieving scalable, robust autonomy across platforms like manipulators, quadrupeds, drones, and mobile robots, each with distinct dynamics, morphologies, and sensing capabilities.

Recent multi-embodiment policy frameworks (e.g., π0, OpenVLA, RT-X) show promising scalability, but their success often hinges on domain-specific adaptation or data-intensive retraining. These approaches rarely address the core question: How do we build policies that generalize structurally and safely across bodies?

We posit that reasoning is a key enabler of trustworthy cross-embodiment policy learning. Structured reasoning modules — grounded in causal world models, intermediate abstractions, or semantic representations — can help robots make decisions that are safe, interpretable, and transferrable across embodiments. Equally important are methods like representation learning, and morphology-aware policy design, which offer scalable mechanisms to align behaviors across diverse agents.

Language, as a medium of abstraction and compositionality, further provides a natural interface for expressing transferable intent, constraints, and affordances. Vision-language and Vision-language-action models (e.g., VLMs, VLAs) hold great potential for bridging embodiment gaps through interpretable goal representation and instruction-following capabilities.

This workshop will investigate one central question: How can we build safe policies that generalize across robotic embodiments using reasoning and related scalable techniques?

Topics of interest include:

  • Structured Reasoning for Transfer: How can world models, causal reasoning, or chain-of-thought methods support cross-embodiment generalization?
  • Safety and Robustness: How do we ensure safe behavior when transferring policies across bodies with different control spaces or constraints?
  • Language and Foundation Models: How can language-based representations enhance policy transfer and interpretability?
  • Imitation Learning and Cross-Embodiment Skill Transfer: What are scalable strategies to align policies across embodiments using demonstrations or distillation?
  • Representation Learning: What embeddings or abstractions best support morphology- and dynamics-invariant policies?
  • Evaluation and Benchmarking: How should we measure safety, trust, and generalization across embodiments in simulation and reality?

Why This Workshop Is Timely and Important
Generalist robot intelligence depends on building policies that transfer safely and effectively across embodiments — a challenge at the heart of scalable real-world deployment. Without structural generalization, every new morphology requires costly retraining, limiting adaptability and trust.

By focusing on reasoning-based, interpretable, and robust methods for cross-embodiment transfer, this workshop aims to foster new directions in robot learning — drawing insights from policy learning, multimodal reasoning, representation learning, and safety-focused design.

Intended Audience
We invite researchers from:
  • Robot Learning and Embodied AI (policy generalization, cross-domain transfer, imitation learning)
  • Reasoning and Foundation Models (VLAs, VLMs, language-action alignment)
  • Safety and Trustworthy AI (safe RL, verifiable learning, robustness)
  • Reinforcement and Imitation Learning (transfer, continual learning, causal RL)
  • Representation Learning (abstractions, shared embeddings, embodiment-aware skill transfer)

We aim to bring together a cross-disciplinary audience from academia and industry to advance the frontiers of generalist, trustworthy, and transferable robot intelligence.
